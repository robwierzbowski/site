A note on interviews:

I love interviews where we talk about the organizational why and technical how of a feature set, pair on existing codebases, explore architecture and ways of working, and take home exercises where I have a chance to fully consider the requirements and edges of a prompt. These are excellent ways for me to display my expertise in technology, architecture, and communication.

I produce at a high rate in a work environment, but because of the breadth of responsibilities at my level it takes me an hour or so to get into a coding mindset. Speed focused and leetcode interviews do not help a potential employer understand what I bring to a company—if this is part of your interview process I suggest we revise that step into a deeper technical discussion or pairing exercise. I will not participate in technical interviews that don’t represent the role being interviewed for (e.g., algorithm quizzing); these tend to be cultural and socioeconomic filters instead of an exploration of the way a candidate thinks about work, and are harmful to our profession.
