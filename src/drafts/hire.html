A note on interviews:

An interview process is a reflection of company culture. I look for interviews that allow me to communicate my expertise in technology, architecture, and leadership. I love an interview where we talk about the organizational why and technical how of a feature set, pair on existing codebases, or explore architecture and ways of working. A take home coding exercise where I can fully consider the requirements and edges of a prompt is also effective.

Because of the breadth of the responsibilities at my level it takes me an hour or so to get into a coding mindset. Leetcode and speed focused exercises do not help a potential employer understand what I bring to a company. If this is a step in your interview process I suggest we replace it with a deeper technical discussion or pairing exercise. I don't participate in technical interviews that are unrelated to the role (e.g., algorithm quizzes); these tend to be cultural and socioeconomic filters instead of an investigation of how a candidate thinks about work, and are harmful to our profession.
